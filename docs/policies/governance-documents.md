# Governance Documents

## Constitution & By-Laws

The GSC is governed by its Constitution and By-Laws. These documents define the structure, roles, voting rules, and procedures for the organization.

:::info Note on current role names
The GSC is governed by its Constitution and By-Laws. These documents remain the formal governing documents linked here. Some officer titles in those documents use older terminology. The current Executive Board’s operating titles and responsibilities are reflected on the Officers pages. Where terminology differs, use the Officers pages for current role names and the Constitution and By-Laws for formal governance rules.
:::

## Resolutions

A Resolution is an official GSC statement on a topic or recommended course of action. To be considered, a resolution must concern the good of graduate students broadly. Article VII of the Constitution outlines the full process for proposing and voting on resolutions.

Notable recent resolutions:
- **International Student Protections** — calling for university protections for international graduate students
- **Endorsing Unionization** — the GSC's position on graduate student union representation

Past resolutions are available on the [GSC website](https://studentorg.vanderbilt.edu/gsc/).

## Student Organization Policies

The GSC operates as a registered student organization at Vanderbilt and is subject to the rules in the [Vanderbilt Student Handbook](https://www.vanderbilt.edu/student_handbook/). This includes:

- All executive board members must complete **Host Responsibility Training**
- Members making purchases must complete **Student Finance Training**
- The GSC must re-register annually through AnchorLink each spring (deadline ~mid-May)

GSC funding comes from student activity fees and is **not subject** to the standard SSF allocation process, though standard SSF spending rules apply.
