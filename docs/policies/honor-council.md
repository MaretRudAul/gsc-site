# Graduate Honor Council (GHC)

The Graduate Honor Council (GHC) is an independent body whose President and Vice President are ex-officio members of the GSC Executive Board per the GSC Constitution. The two organizations operate independently, but the GSC regularly supports the GHC through advocacy and financial support for events and outreach.

---

## What is the GHC?

The GHC oversees academic integrity for graduate students at Vanderbilt. It handles cases of academic dishonesty and trains honor council volunteers from across the graduate student community.

:::tip Get involved
The GSC President is encouraged to volunteer for the Honor Council and go through volunteer training. It's a useful way to understand how academic integrity works at the graduate level.
:::

## Annual events

- **Welcome/orientation for incoming grad students** — GHC presents during fall orientation to advertise the honor system
- **Cross-campus honor council meeting** — Annual meeting of all Vanderbilt honor councils (usually in spring; GHC receives email invitation)

---

## For graduate students

If you have a question about academic integrity policies or need to report a concern, contact the GHC or the Office of Academic Affairs directly. The GHC operates independently of the GSC — contact them directly for honor code matters.
