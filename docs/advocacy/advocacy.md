# Advocacy

The Graduate Student Council advocates for policies that benefit the graduate student body. We represent graduate student concerns, facilitate communication between graduate students, administration, and faculty, and use meetings, committees, resolutions, and survey data to move issues forward.

## How GSC advocacy works
The GSC’s advocacy work happens through regular communication with university leadership and graduate-student-serving offices. GSC leadership meets monthly with Dean André Christie-Mizell and the Graduate School, works regularly with the Dean of Students Office, and participates in recurring meetings with GPAS, the Graduate Development Network (GDN), and the SEC Graduate Leader Coalition. Provost Cybele Raver typically joins the Dean meeting once per semester.

## Bring a concern to the GSC
If you have a graduate-student concern, email gsc@vanderbilt.edu . The GSC exists to help bring graduate student concerns forward and to connect students with the appropriate university offices when broader advocacy or coordination is needed.

## Advocacy tools
The GSC uses several tools to support advocacy work: official council resolutions, participation in university committees and working groups, recurring meetings with administrators, and the independent GSC Graduate Student Survey.
