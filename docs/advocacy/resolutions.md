# Resolutions

A Resolution is a statement issued by the Graduate Student Council stating its official position or recommended course of action on a topic or issue. Resolutions should concern the good of graduate students at large, or at least a significant constituency of graduate students.

## How to propose a resolution
To demonstrate the general nature of a proposed resolution, it should be sponsored by at least three co-submitters. Resolutions should first be sent to the GSC President via gsc@vanderbilt.edu . The President will inform the co-submitters of the next council meeting, and one of the co-submitters must be present at that meeting to read and explain the resolution.

## How resolutions are considered
All Graduate Student Council representatives will have the opportunity to discuss and revise a resolution if needed. Once discussion is complete, the Council votes on the final resolution. Resolutions may be approved by a majority vote of the Council at any meeting. If approved, either the President of the GSC or the submitter of the resolution will forward it to the appropriate administrative body or official, and the approved resolution will carry the endorsement of the Graduate Student Council.

## Personal concerns vs. council resolutions
The Council should not vote on resolutions based on individual concerns. Graduate students with personal concerns should contact a GSC officer directly.

## Examples of prior resolutions
Examples of prior resolutions can be found on the GSC website. Of particular note in the transition document are the International Student Protections and Endorsing Unionization resolutions.
