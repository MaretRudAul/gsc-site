# Mission & Role

## Who we are

The Graduate School, and thus the GSC, serves some research-focused master’s programs and all Ph.D. programs at Vanderbilt University. We represent approximately **2,400 graduate students**.

## What we do

The GSC has two primary goals:

**1. Community & events**
We coordinate academic, social, and other activities significant to the graduate community. We strive to promote community among graduate students and facilitate integration into the broader university community. All graduate students are welcome at our meetings, events, and socials.

**2. Advocacy & representation**
We ascertain and represent graduate student opinions and concerns, facilitating communication between graduate students, administration, and faculty — and advocating for policies that benefit the graduate student body.

## Structure

The GSC is governed by its [Constitution and By-Laws](/docs/policies/governance-documents). It consists of:

- An **Executive Board** (elected positions — see [Executive Board](/docs/officers/executive-board))
- A **voting general body** of department representatives and at-large members (see [Department Representatives](/docs/officers/representatives))
- All currently enrolled graduate students as non-voting general members

## Funding

The GSC receives an automatic annual allocation from student activity fees — **$73,392 in 2024–25**. This funding supports events, travel grants, co-sponsorships, and operational costs. See [Funding Overview](/docs/resources/funding-overview) for details.

## How to participate

- Attend a **General Body Meeting** — first Tuesday of each month, 6–7pm, Alumni Hall 201 (also on Zoom)
- Become your **department's representative** — contact your Director of Graduate Studies
- Come to **social events** — watch for emails and Instagram announcements
- Reach out at [gsc@vanderbilt.edu](mailto:gsc@vanderbilt.edu)

---

## University relationships

The GSC works primarily with:

- **The Graduate School** — primary liaison; Dean André Christie-Mizell meets monthly with GSC leadership
- **Dean of Students Office** — student organization support, AnchorLink, and funding
- **The Provost's Office** — GSC President and VPs meet with Provost Raver once per semester
- **Faculty Senate Student Life Committee** — for matters affecting graduate student policies

The GSC also participates in the **SEC Graduate Leader Coalition**, a network of graduate student governments across SEC schools that meets monthly.
