# Executive Board

The GSC Executive Board is elected each spring and serves for the following academic year.

:::info Who can attend our meetings?
All graduate students are welcome to attend GSC General Body Meetings. You do not need to be a representative or officer to participate.
:::

## Executive Board Roles at a Glance

| Role | Key responsibilities |
|------|---------------------|
| President | Agenda, GBM/exec, admin liaison, provost meetings |
| VP External Affairs | Grad School, GDN, SEC Coalition, GPAS |
| VP Internal Affairs | Dept reps, committees, orgs, newsletters |
| Treasurer | Purchases, budget, faculty advisor approvals |
| Secretary | Minutes, AnchorLink, communications |
| Academic Chairs (×2) | Travel grants, 3MT |
| Community Chairs (×2) | Dinner with Dores, community programs |
| Social Chairs (×2) | Parties, social events |
| PR Chair | Social media, website |

## Faculty Advisor

**Katherine L. Friedman, Ph.D.** — Professor and Vice Chair of Biological Sciences

Kathy approves all expenditures and advises the Executive Board. She typically attends executive board meetings but not general body meetings (unless invited).

## Honor Council Representatives

The Graduate Honor Council President and Vice President are ex-officio members of the GSC Executive Board per the GSC Constitution. See [Honor Council](/docs/policies/honor-council).
