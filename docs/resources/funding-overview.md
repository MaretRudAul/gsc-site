# Funding for Students & Orgs

The GSC receives an annual allocation from student activity fees (~$73,000 in 2024–25) and uses it to support graduate students and registered student organizations.

:::tip Who is eligible?
All currently enrolled graduate students (in the Graduate School) are eligible for individual funding programs. Co-sponsorships are for **registered graduate student organizations** only.
:::

---

## Travel Grant {#travel-grants}

Monthly lottery funding for graduate students attending academic conferences or professional development events.

**Amount:** Varies · **Frequency:** Monthly (August–May, 10 awards per year)

**How to apply:**
1. Go to the [Travel Grant application form](https://studentorg.vanderbilt.edu/gsc/travel-funding-request/) *(updates each year — check for the current form)*
2. Submit your information before the monthly deadline
3. Winners are selected by lottery from eligible applicants
4. If selected and eligible, you'll receive an email — check `gsc_academic@vanderbilt.edu` sent items for the format

**Eligibility check:** You must be currently enrolled in the Graduate School and not have received a GSC travel grant recently. The academic chairs verify eligibility before awarding.

[Apply for a Travel Grant →](https://studentorg.vanderbilt.edu/gsc/travel-funding-request/)

## Co-Sponsorships {#co-sponsorships}

The GSC co-sponsors events and activities organized by other registered student groups that serve the graduate student community.

**Who can apply:** Registered student organizations serving graduate students.

**How it works:**
1. Submit a co-sponsorship request via AnchorLink
2. The GSC reviews and votes on requests at the next executive board or general body meeting
3. Allow **2–3 weeks** for the full approval process — plan ahead
4. Purchases follow the same student org finance rules as all GSC spending

**Contact:** [gsc_community@vanderbilt.edu](mailto:gsc_community@vanderbilt.edu)

[Request a Co-Sponsorship →](https://anchorlink.vanderbilt.edu/organization/GSC)

## Graduate & Professional Student Org Funding

Separately from GSC's own funds, the GSC President chairs the **Student Organization Funding Committee** — a university-wide committee that recommends annual funding allocations for all graduate and professional student organizations.

Applications open in the spring through AnchorLink. Contact [gsc@vanderbilt.edu](mailto:gsc@vanderbilt.edu) for more information about this process.

## Other Funding Sources at Vanderbilt

The GSC is not the only source of funding for graduate students. Other programs include:

| Program | Administered by | For |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| [Graduate School Conference Travel Grant](https://gradschool.vanderbilt.edu/) | Graduate School / GLI | Conference travel |
| [Dissertation Enhancement Grant](https://gradschool.vanderbilt.edu/) | Graduate School / GLI | Dissertation research costs |
| [Professional Development & Training Grant](https://gradschool.vanderbilt.edu/) | Graduate School / GLI | Skills and career training |
| [Student Critical Support Fund](https://www.vanderbilt.edu/deanofstudents/) | Dean of Students | Financial hardship |
| [Student Care Assistance Program (SCAP)](https://www.vanderbilt.edu/deanofstudents/) | Dean of Students | Medical/mental health costs |

:::note About SCAP and the Critical Support Fund
These programs have significant eligibility restrictions. In particular, the Critical Support Fund cannot reimburse costs already paid — you must apply *before* the expense. Contact the Dean of Students for current details.
:::
